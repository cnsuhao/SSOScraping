Instructions</b></span></font><br>\n<font color=\"#000000\"><span style=\"font-family: Times New Roman\"><font size=\"2\"><i>The following tutorial was taken from Kick *** Quilts. The original tutorial is courtesy of Patrick McKenna, and is posted here with his permission. This tutorial is also available in PDF format from <a href=\"http://teamgunnparker.com/downloads.php?cat_id=1\" target=\"_blank\">here</a> or <a href=\"http://www.angrysparrow.com/hammock_info/kickass_quilt.pdf\" target=\"_blank\">here</a> (thanks gun parker and angrysparrow). <a href=\"http://www.adobe.com/products/acrobat/readstep2.html?promoid=BUIGO\" target=\"_blank\">Acrobat Reader</a> is required to view files. Now on to the good stuff....</i></font></span></font><br>\n<br>\n<br>\n<font color=\"#000000\"><span style=\"font-family: Times New Roman\">I think making plans available is the right thing to do for a variety of reasons. I also think it's a useful way of showing you what you're buying from the inside out.</span></font><br>\n<br>\n<font color=\"#000000\"><span style=\"font-family: Times New Roman\">I'm going to give instructions on how to make mine exactly. I highly encourage you to embellish on the design and/or materials to suit your own needs, though. Here are some possible variations:</span></font><br>\n<br>\n<b>Ultra-lightYou could use 0.8 oz. ripstop instead of 1.1 oz. You could use less insulation, maybe have it only double-layered in key areas, like around the torso and shoulders. You could leave off the reinforcing patches and side tie-out loops. For the corner loops, you could use 1/4\" Grosgrain ribbon instead of 3/4\" and leave off the cordlocks and just use knots instead. The 0.8 oz. will save you several ounces and the insulation skimping should save at least that much.<br>\n<br>\n<b>Heavy duty</b><br>\nYou could use 1.9 oz. ripstop breathable and 330 denier reinforcing patches. You could use nylon straps instead of the Grosgrain ribbon. You could reinforce all seams with Grosgrain.
